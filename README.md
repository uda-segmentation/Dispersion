# Unsupervised Domain Adaptation for Semantic Segmentation Based on Instance Directional Dispersion

## Overview

This work addresses unsupervised domain adaptation for semantic segmentation, targeting the challenge of transferring knowledge from synthetic to real-world domains by leveraging stable geometric relationships among semantic instances. We observe that the relative directional dispersion of semantic class instances exhibits consistent spatial patterns across domains, and propose to explicitly model this dispersion as a geometric cue—complementary to or in lieu of conventional depth information. Within a multi-task learning framework, directional dispersion is encoded via auxiliary feature maps and fused with depth to form a dual-modality geometric representation. This enriched representation is further employed to guide pixel-wise pseudo-label weighting during self-training, enhancing the reliability and robustness of target-domain supervision. When incorporated into state-of-the-art UDA backbones (DAFormer, HRDA, and MIC), our approach yields consistent mIoU improvements: +2.3%, +2.0%, and +3.0% on Synthia→Cityscapes, and +1.9%, +1.3%, and +1.2% on GTA→Cityscapes, respectively, demonstrating the effectiveness and generality of directional dispersion geometry for cross-domain semantic segmentation.
